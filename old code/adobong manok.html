Adobong Manok

History

Adobong Manok is one of the most iconic dishes in Filipino cuisine. Its roots date back to pre-Hispanic times when Filipinos preserved food using vinegar and salt. When the Spanish colonizers arrived in the 16th century, they introduced soy sauce, giving the dish its signature salty and savory flavor. The term "adobo" comes from the Spanish word "adobar," which means "to marinate." Over time, Adobong Manok evolved into a rich and flavorful dish, combining sour, salty, and umami flavors, making it a beloved comfort food for Filipinos worldwide.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into serving pieces)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic (minced)
  • 1 teaspoon peppercorns
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • Optional: 1 tablespoon sugar (for a sweeter flavor)

Procedures

  1. In a bowl, marinate the chicken in soy sauce and minced garlic for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat cooking oil in a pan and saut√© the marinated chicken until lightly browned.
  3. Add the marinade, vinegar, peppercorns, and bay leaves to the pan.
  4. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer for 20-30 minutes, or until the chicken is tender.
  5. Stir occasionally and add water if needed to prevent drying out.
  6. Optional: Add sugar for a hint of sweetness and adjust seasoning to taste.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice.
